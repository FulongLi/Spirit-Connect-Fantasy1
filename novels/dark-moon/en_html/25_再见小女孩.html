23 Goodbye, Little Girl

"The order in which events unfolded no longer seemed important. What mattered was that the information had always been there."

Meeting the mysterious little girl again

Too much time had passed, so long that I began to suspect the electronic screen outside the monitoring room had frozen on the same line of data.

Anna, Charlie, and the dozen or so people who had undergone correction that day all lay inside Spirit Connect pods, a row of silver-white shells as quiet as props. Sometimes I stood outside the glass and looked at that row of pods, and a ridiculous thought would jump into my mind: had they already been silently replaced by another batch, and no one had told me?

After that, I applied to log into Another World's "Moon-Dark Fragment." Officially, it was to cooperate with post-traumatic evaluation data. In reality, even Dr. Lune knew perfectly well: I wanted to try once more and find that little girl.

First time, second time, third time... In the game, the stone gate, the ancient battlefield, the yellow-sand horizon fragments, everything ran according to script. I stood before that enormous "stone gate" like an orphan lost inside an art scene, somehow walking out the deja vu of a patrol route.

She was not there.

At least from the game layer, no frequency "movement" could be seen. After one inspection, Dr. Lune held his tablet and half-joked, "You look like you are calling out to an NPC whose account has been deleted."

"Possible," I said. "But it is also possible that this instance layer of yours is too thick."

He had nothing to say.

Until one night, when the institute had only night-shift lights left. My own shadow was reflected on the big glass wall of the monitoring room, with dark circles under the eyes and messy hair, nothing like an eighteen-year-old.

When I lay down in the Spirit Connect pod, for one instant I understood very clearly: if I still could not contact her this time, there might truly be no chance. Perhaps everything before had been coincidence.

I put on the haptic suit. The hatch closed. Familiar weightlessness.

The system started. The Another World logo appeared at the center of my vision, then slowly faded. Main menu, settings, characters, world list... I knew it so well I no longer needed to look. My gesture directly pulled out the hidden item and pressed:

Moon-Dark Fragment

Status: Internal Test · Authorized Accounts Only

The image went black. The stone gate appeared.

Everything looked the same as before: huge door panels, dense patterns, a passage in the distance whose end could not be seen. Only one detail was wrong. The sound was too clean.

There was no engine-preset wind, no footsteps, no environmental background noise, only an extremely light, thin "blankness," like the dead silence one second before recording begins. I stood before the gate, did not push it, did not press any virtual key, only silently repeated in my mind: "If you can hear me, this is the last time this week. If there is still no response, I will first... stop for a while."

The patterns on the stone gate trembled slightly. It was not the uniform "texture animation" in the program, but a finer shiver, like ripples spreading outward from the inside.

Immediately afterward, familiar blue-black noise points seeped from the edges of my vision, like someone dripping a drop of ink onto a pure white canvas, the ink lazily spreading outward.

Then a voice fell in: "Unstable..."

The voice did not seem to enter through my ears. It was more like someone had written a very fine line of words deep inside my consciousness. It was still her, but farther and thinner than when I heard her in the frequency horizon last time, as if separated by several walls.

"You... I finally connected to you." I pressed down my heartbeat and tried to speak steadily. "I want..."

"I know what you want to ask."

She interrupted. "Many consciousness-stream clusters on your side have not returned. Do not waste time."

I froze. "On your side... is something happening too?"

"Yes."

"The connection where we currently are is unstable. The connection window is very short. There is too much signal noise in this simulation space of yours. I cannot remain long." She did not speak quickly, yet there was a pressure in her voice that made one automatically hold one's breath. It was unlike when I had seen her before. Then she had spoken in a field she controlled, and even without explanation, she had not been rushed.

"Then I will be brief." I took a deep breath. "Anna, Charlie, and the others. Can you tell me where they are?"

"The reason your correction accident was triggered is that Spirit Connect S&T stored consciousness backup data in near-Earth orbital space data centers, making it possible for your consciousness waves to couple with the lunar node. Their consciousnesses were pulled into their own frequency horizons. They are now like children drowning in a sea of memory. From my angle, they have not 'disappeared.' They are each trapped in their own universe, just as if I had not pulled you out of the Terasha Record before, you would have drowned like them. And the lunar guards are now filtering out their consciousnesses and crushing them..."

"Wait, wait..." I felt my heartbeat leap into my throat. "You mean they will be... 'deleted'?"

"'Deleted' is the word you are used to using," she corrected calmly. "The more accurate term is... deconstruction."

"They will be dismantled into unstructured frequency noise. No pain, no sense of process, and no self-awareness of the instant of 'ending.' As individuals, they will no longer exist. But the information that composes them will not be wasted." She said this string of words too calmly, so calmly that I felt cold instead.

"What about you?" I gritted my teeth. "Can't you stop them? Last time, didn't you pull me out of the Terasha Record?"

The patterns on the stone gate suddenly dimmed for an instant, as if some occlusion pressed down from far away.

She was silent for several seconds before speaking. "In certain regions, I can make 'annotations.' Occasionally, I can temporarily extract individual entities. But now is different."

For the first time, her voice carried a rare tightness. "The level I am currently in has been brought back under supervision by a higher protocol. Many things I could quietly do in the past now leave traces. This collective overflow on your side has already been regarded as an 'abnormal fluctuation event.' Before these events are fully archived, what I can do is very limited."

"Then at least..." I tried hard to suppress the anxiety in my heart. "At least can you tell me how much time they have left?"

"For you?" she asked faintly. "For the side where the bodies are still breathing?"

"Yes," I said.

A brief silence.

I could almost feel that in some dimension we could not observe at all, she was rapidly comparing something. Finally, she gave an answer. "After their consciousnesses are crushed, their bodies will become what you call 'vegetative.' They will never wake. Time has no meaning for an unconscious body."
