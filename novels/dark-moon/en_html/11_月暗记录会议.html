09 The Lunar Record Meeting

The meeting begins

Monday arrived quickly.

In the end, Jim pressed "Attend" on the confirmation key. The meeting room was on the top floor of the institute's main building. The entire floor-to-ceiling window had been covered by a holographic mask, flattening the city's nightscape outside into a faint contour line. Only the ring of cold white light around the tabletop remained, making every face look somewhat pale.

Seated on both sides of the long table were the heads of various departments, and several people Jim had never seen before who clearly did not belong to the Spirit Connect S&T system: one with a military bearing, another from the administrative system, and an old man who looked somewhat out of place.

The old man had white hair and beard, wore old-style metal-framed glasses, and had a thick stack of paper materials spread out in front of him, the corners worn and curled from repeated turning. His nameplate read: Jared McGrath, historian of ancient civilizations and theologian.

Dr. Lune sat slightly to the side of the main seat, in a subtle position. In the main seat sat an elderly man with gray temples but vigorous spirits. The nameplate before him listed no title, only a name: Elias Speer. Everyone present, however, called him "Mr. Speer."

Jim's gaze fell on the old man in the main seat, and a strange feeling suddenly rose in his heart. It was not worship, nor simple familiarity. It was more like some memory that did not belong to him had suddenly matched a face. The feeling came for no reason, yet it was extraordinarily strong, like an invisible thread quietly pulled taut between them.

Jim frowned and tried to press down this strange emotion. He had never met Mr. Speer, at least not in his memory. But why did he feel this?

He shook his head and temporarily pushed the thought aside. Now was not the time.

"Then..."

Mr. Speer glanced at the time and closed the file in his hand.

"Place the magnetic disk on the center of your brow. Let us begin."

He raised his head and set his gaze on Jim. "Jim, we have already read part of your record. But there is one section, the information projection given to you by that mysterious little girl, that has no complete system narrative, and appears to be an information structure that current digital AI cannot parse. You mentioned that the information involves the birth of the Moon, Earth's earlier civilizations, and the origin of life."

He paused. "Today, I hope you can recount it as completely as possible according to what you saw. Do not rush to explain. Just describe."

The air sank noticeably.

Jim felt his throat go dry and instinctively looked toward Dr. Lune beside him.

Dr. Lune gave him a slight nod, his eyes seeming to say: take your time, at your own pace.

Jim took a deep breath and prepared to speak, but his gaze drifted again toward the main seat without his intending it. Mr. Speer did not urge him. He only pressed the file in his hand slightly downward, as if telling him: no hurry.

The three-dimensional projection in the center of the conference table lit up. This too was a product unique to Spirit Connect S&T. A speaker wearing the magnetic disk could have images generated in real time according to their consciousness. Immediately, a slowly rotating model of Earth hovered above everyone.
