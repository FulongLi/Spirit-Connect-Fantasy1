30 Landing on the Dark Side of the Moon

The Moon gradually grew larger in the porthole. It was not the romantic white sphere of movies, but a cold, hard celestial body covered in shadows and impact scars. When the spacecraft orbited to the dark side, Earth's light disappeared completely.

The dark side had no navigation beacons, no external light sources for reference. The spacecraft relied entirely on internal models and preset paths, like walking the final stretch with eyes closed.

"Entering dark-side orbit. Attitude correction complete."

Jim could feel an extremely subtle change. Not in the body, but in consciousness. As if something were watching him from the dark.

The landing point was in an area never publicly marked. There were no traces left by humans there, only terrain forever untouched by sunlight, rocks like frozen waves.

"Landing procedure initiated."

The spacecraft slowly descended, jet flow silently unfolding in vacuum. At the instant of touchdown, there was no vibration. Only one line of text appeared in Jim's vision:

Location Confirmed: Dark Side of the Moon

On the control-room side, all monitoring data stabilized at that moment.

"He has arrived," Dr. Lune said softly.

Speer closed his eyes, as if confirming a node from which there was no return. Professor Cross simply stood quietly, like a coordinate forgotten by time. On Dr. Sean's terminal, the frequency horizon model began reconstructing itself automatically. Inside Jim's cabin, the lights slowly dimmed.

The hatch did not open immediately. Before external contact was completely cut, Dr. Lune's voice came one last time. "Jim, we are here."

Jim did not answer.

He only looked through the porthole at that eternal darkness outside, and in his heart the familiar and distant voice suddenly surfaced.

"There is not much time."

And this time, he had finally arrived in the true sense at the place she had named: the dark side of the Moon.
