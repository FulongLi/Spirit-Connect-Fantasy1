11 Humanity Is the Host

Where do humans come from?

After throwing out the question, he looked at Jim, then turned his head slightly toward Jared. "You two can speak from different angles."

Jim froze slightly, understanding in his heart: the historian of ancient civilizations had clearly not been invited merely to listen to a story. He inhaled softly, and the three-dimensional projection shifted into the oceans and continents of ancient Earth.

"First, the body."

"There is no dispute that the human body comes from Earth's own life system. From the fertilized egg constantly dividing and differentiating until the infant is born, the embryonic development of each of us actually compresses and reenacts the entire history of life's evolution inside the female womb..."

In the projection, the skeletons and outlines of single-celled life, multicellular life, fish, amphibians, reptiles, mammals, and primates flashed past one after another.

"Organic molecules in the ocean gradually organized into cells, and cells assembled into multicellular bodies. Then fish came onto land, amphibians differentiated into reptiles, and certain lineages gradually evolved into mammals and primates. From a biochemical perspective, this path is very 'reasonable.'"

He paused, his tone slowing a little. "But this path has an upper limit: at most, it can produce extremely complex instincts, emotions, and limited reasoning. It cannot naturally cross into abstract thought, symbolic systems, self-consciousness, time-consciousness, or death-consciousness in the sense we now mean."

He enlarged the skeletal image of an ape.

"At the ape stage, the bodily structure was already complex enough, and the cerebral cortex had enormous potential. But it still lacked one condition: an architecture capable of containing, binding, and stably driving 'non-material consciousness.'"

"At that point in time..."

Jim stared at the projection and said slowly, "I saw a civilization arrive on Earth."

For the moment, the projection showed no spacecraft, only strings of unfamiliar parameters and biological model structures.

"They did not descend onto the tops of pyramids as movies imagine, nor did they appear in ancient paintings holding scepters. They were more like a group of experimental engineers. They came not to conquer, nor for resources, but to intervene in the evolution of consciousness."

Someone drew in a breath.

"They screened all the large species on Earth, looking for the structure most suitable as a 'consciousness carrier.' At first, they tried whales: huge brains, developed neural networks, complex social behavior."

At this point, an enormous shadow suddenly swept through the depth of the projection. It was like a whale, yet unlike any whale known to humans today. The shadow appeared for less than a second before the image automatically pressed it back into the background of the primitive ocean.

Jim himself froze.

"What was that?" someone asked.

"I don't know," Jim said quickly. "She did not explain it to me. Only... there had once been something very large there."

If Charlie had been here, he would probably have made a joke at a completely inappropriate moment. Thinking of that, Jim's chest suddenly sank.

He pulled his attention back and continued. "But whales had one fatal problem: they cannot leave the sea, and therefore cannot become a 'global diffusion node.' A consciousness experiment requires a carrier that interacts maximally with its environment."

He tapped the table, and the image switched to upright-walking primates. "Upright posture, freed hands, forward-facing eyes, strong sociality, sufficient lifespan, an extremely developed nervous system... Structurally, apes possessed all the prerequisites for becoming vessels of consciousness. So they rewrote genes. They did not 'create' humans. They turned a bend and rewrote the direction of evolution."

A complex genetic map and brain neural structure appeared on the projection.

"They strengthened the nervous system, extended the development cycle, and especially modified the structure of the prefrontal cortex, making it better at processing abstract language and symbols. But the real key was not intelligence."

The projection highlighted a small special network structure.

"They embedded a fragment into the genes, a neural resonance network that would ultimately be able to produce, sustain, and synchronize with external frequencies. Its function was not to 'think,' but to contain and bind an independent unit of consciousness."

At the far end of the conference table, someone said almost inaudibly, "...a soul...?"

This time, it was Jared, the old scholar, who spoke.

He gently pushed up his glasses. His voice was not loud, but steady. "In many ancient civilizational systems, this 'resonance network' is described as 'spirit' or 'soul.' They did not have the language of neuroscience. They could only use metaphor."

He paused, as if organizing his words. "In Chinese civilization, 'hun' and 'po' are two different concepts. 'Hun' is yang, connected to heaven, the core of consciousness; 'po' is yin, connected to earth, the function of the body. After a person dies, the 'hun' ascends to heaven, and the 'po' returns to earth. This dual structure is very likely a blurred memory of the 'resonance network': the separation of consciousness and carrier."

"In ancient Egyptian civilization, 'Ka' is life force, 'Ba' is personality, and 'Akh' is the eternal soul. Only when the three are combined does a complete person exist. This triadic structure may correspond to different layers of consciousness."

"In Hinduism, 'Atman' is the eternal soul. It is trapped in 'Samsara,' reincarnating again and again. Only when it obtains liberation, 'Moksha,' can it return to 'Brahman.' This concept of being 'trapped' and 'returning' is astonishingly consistent with what Jim just said about 'containing and binding an independent unit of consciousness.'"

"In Sumerian civilization, humans are described as 'servants of the gods,' and their souls come from 'the blood of the gods.' But in older texts, the human soul is 'placed,' not 'created.'"

In an instant, everyone's gaze focused on him. Jared smiled faintly and nodded. "Sorry. Occupational habit. I interrupted. Please continue."

Jim gave him a slight nod, then looked back at the others. "They did not give intelligence to humans. They built a usable vehicle for the 'soul.' Standing upright, using tools, language: all of these were side effects of the modification. The true key event was that the body, for the first time, gained the ability to contain non-material consciousness."

He paused, as if pulling himself away from the little girl's perspective. "This is not myth, not 'being abducted by aliens,' and not a religious story. It was bioengineering, a cross-civilizational consciousness transplant project."
