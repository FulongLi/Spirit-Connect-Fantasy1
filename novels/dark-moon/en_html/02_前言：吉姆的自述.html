Prologue: Jim's Statement

Countless legends circulate about the dark side of the Moon. Some say a deliberately buried history is hidden there. Others say that if one can find and unravel its secrets, one can understand the true operating rules of this world.

I had read such claims in books since childhood.

Perhaps for that reason, I developed a sustained, almost stubborn interest in cosmology and social structures. I wanted to know how civilizations are born, how order forms, and why truth is always hidden beneath layer after layer of covering.

My name is Jim Vale, born in the New Century. If you come from my past, then I am your junior. If you come from my future, I may also count as your predecessor.

But none of that matters.

Time is not the criterion of identity. What we share is the same immense cosmic narrative, and the individual is only a memory node briefly awakened within that narrative.

When information begins to overflow, when logic forms multiple loops, when cause and effect no longer converge on a single result, you will realize that some knowledge does not exist for the sake of being transmitted.

So when you truly know everything, about Earth, the home we once shared; about the origin and endpoint of civilization; about the universe not being a natural evolution but more like a rule system being executed, how will you choose?

Will you run out and tell everyone, trying to awaken your family and friends? Will you keep silent? Or will you weave a gentle and necessary lie, letting the world continue to run along its predetermined path?

There is no single answer. But the choice itself will define your position.

-- Jim's statement
