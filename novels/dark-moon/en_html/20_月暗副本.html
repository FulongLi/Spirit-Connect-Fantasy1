18 The Moon-Dark Instance

Wake up soon, my friends

"Were you thinking about your childhood?" Dr. Lune's voice pulled me back from memory. Only then did I realize I had been staring at Anna's hatch for a very long time. In the reflection on the glass, I saw a blurred version of myself, much taller than when I was a child, and much more exhausted.

"Mm," I said. "I was thinking, when we first played Another World, if I hadn't blocked that blow..."

"That recommendation letter would not have been written for Anna," Dr. Lune continued for me. "You might not have formed that kind of team."

"How did you figure that out?" I asked instinctively.

He lifted the tablet in his hand. "We have your behavior records from back then."

"You kept even those?" I was a little surprised.

"You were test-server participants at the time," he shrugged. "Your behavior in any system was important sample data to them, to Mr. Speer."

He paused, then added, "To me as well."

"Me?" I smiled. "Wasn't I just the impulsive one who charged forward every time?"

"Not only impulsive." Dr. Lune shook his head. "You have a very high 'nonessential intervention tendency.'"

"That doesn't sound good." I frowned.

"From a system perspective, it is a very dangerous trait," he said calmly. "You act in places where you do not need to take responsibility, make decisions where you are not required to decide, and voluntarily bear consequences where you do not have to bear them."

"Sounds like... meddling." I sighed.

"It sounds like..." He looked at me. "Someone unsuitable to be a passive observation subject."

I froze.

"And precisely because of that," Dr. Lune said slowly, "when you went to a place like the dark side of the Moon, you had a chance to return."

"What about Anna?" I could not help asking. "What is she in the eyes of the system?"

Dr. Lune thought for a moment. "She is the kind who, once she knows there are 'rules,' must find a way to see the full picture of those rules. She is not satisfied with being a player. She is always testing whether she can become a 'reader of the system.'"

"And Charlie?"

"Charlie..." Dr. Lune smiled. "He is the one most like an ordinary person, but often makes the most extraordinary choices at key nodes. He can be afraid of death, hesitate, mutter endlessly, but when it is truly his turn, he rarely retreats."

I leaned back against the railing and looked at the two quiet Spirit Connect pods.

"Maybe that's why we grew up together," I said softly. "If only one person were left, probably none of us would have reached this point."

Dr. Lune said nothing. He only looked toward the pod room as well.

Inside Anna's pod, the light patterns blinked rhythmically like breathing. On Charlie's side, a faint fluctuation occasionally appeared on the monitoring interface, like him talking in his sleep. I suddenly realized that from a very early time, we had been used to facing the unknown together in another world.

Only this time, the unknown was no longer a level designed by the system, but a universe that may have treated us as experimental samples from the very beginning.

"When they wake up," I said, "I think... we will probably have to form a team again."

"This time not in a game," Dr. Lune said softly.

"Mm." I nodded. "This time in..."

I stopped, thought for a moment, and still spoke that name. "...in the true 'Another World.'"

"Do you hope to turn your journey to the dark side of the Moon into an area inside Another World?" Dr. Lune asked tentatively.

"If so, other players could experience it too. Perhaps there will be new discoveries."

"Yes, no problem. I also want to experience it again."

Digitizing the Moon-dark region

The following section is an excerpt from an internal meeting record I received later.

The first time I read it, I felt very strange. It was clearly discussing how to develop a game area, yet every paragraph felt like dismantling my own dream into parts, labeling them, then installing them back into an artificial box.

Dr. Lune: Welcome to this internal project development group discussion. I believe everyone has read the proposal in advance. What I am going to discuss today is not only "what exists on the dark side of the Moon," but how we will bury it inside Another World as an Easter-egg region no one has yet triggered.

He paused, as if reminding himself not to say too much.

Dr. Lune: First, one thing to give everyone a unified psychological expectation: so far, apart from Jim, no one has truly "linked" to the real Moon-dark again. What may be accessed in the game in the future is only a reconstructed Moon-dark instance based on Jim's memory, of course also including our understanding of the Moon-dark. It is a high-fidelity simulation level, not a true entrance.

Someone asked, "Then are we manufacturing a misleading entrance?"

Dr. Lune: Yes. So the risk of this project is not whether players will enter, but whether after entering, they will think they understand the real Moon-dark. Please remember: what we are reconstructing is the shadow, not the body.

I. What is the dark side of the Moon? And why can it be "simulated"?

First, clarify a concept that is easy to confuse: the "dark side of the Moon" is not simply the "far side of the Moon." From the information Jim brought back, and from later spectral analysis, it is more like a manufactured independent space, a pocket realm. The "entrance" to this space is indeed anchored on the physical far side of the Moon, but the entrance itself is not a hole, a crack, or a wormhole. It is a coordinate capable of resonating with consciousness waves at 4-8 Hz.

At this point, the record contained a blank of several seconds, as if someone in the meeting room had interrupted him.

The project lead asked, "When you say 'capable of resonating,' is that a confirmed value or a guess?"

Dr. Lune: For now, we can only call it a high-confidence guess. We do not have a second sample, nor a second successful return. To enter the true dark side of the Moon, at least two conditions must be satisfied simultaneously: knowing the coordinate, that is, knowing at which "consciousness-space point" to initiate coupling; and providing enough energy to the target consciousness wave so it can climb above that resonance threshold.

The "Moon-dark region" in the game is not directly connected to this entrance. Rather, we used Jim's full-session brainwaves, perceptions, and semantic records inside the Spirit Connect pod to construct a model, then translated it into structures renderable by Another World. That is the "Moon-dark" you can reach in the game. The real Moon-dark exists in higher dimensions. The game level is its "shadow" in our engine coordinate system.

II. Regional structure overview: from the stone gate to the spacetime well

Following the order of Jim's experience, I standardized the naming. The main sections of the dark side of the Moon include: ancient battlefield ruins behind the stone-gate entrance, the yellow-sand horizon, the rice-field horizon, the frequency horizon (outer edge of the Terasha Record), the white horizon, the black horizon, and the spacetime well.

In the game, all seven regions have been made into accessible levels and sublevels. But everyone should remember: only around the white horizon, the frequency horizon, and the black horizon have we observed true "higher-order information traces." The other regions currently seem more like "background scenes and potential clues."

Below, I will explain by region how we "restored" them and what we believe they mean in the real Moon-dark.

III. The stone gate and the ancient battlefield ruins

The real stone gate is very special. Judging from Jim's touch, pressure perception, and action-feedback curves, it is not an ordinary solid. It is closer to a consciousness projection structure superimposed on a two-dimensional hydrogen-metal substrate:

Physical substrate: extremely light, extremely stable, almost without inertia;

Apparent material: changes according to individual cognition.

For Jim, he saw a "huge stone gate." For you, it might be a wooden door, a metal door, or even a light door, depending on your cultural experience. The only constant is that no matter how heavy it looks, it is very light.

We preserved this mechanism in the game as well:

Rendering: the client dynamically selects the door's material and texture according to the player's behavior pattern, personality tags, and cultural preferences;

Interaction: no matter how heavy the door appears, the interaction feedback is light.

This is what we consider the first filter: only those who can "believe" the door can be opened will attempt to push it. Those crushed by their own fear and imagination will stop here.

The ancient battlefield ruins behind the stone gate are difficult to classify simply as "consciousness projection" in reality. We did one thing: conducted deep comparisons between Jim's past memories, reading, films, television, and game experiences and this battlefield. The result showed that he had never encountered any civilizational image highly similar to that ancient battlefield.

This means two things: the ancient battlefield is very likely not a collage produced by his subconscious; it is closer to a historical fragment that truly existed, embedded in the Moon-dark. Judging from weapon forms and residual spectra, some blades are extremely close to the structure of Earth's bronze artifacts. But at present we cannot prove direct lineage between the two. We can only say the similarity is high enough that it "must continue to be tracked."

In the game, we made this segment almost a "panoramic replica": weapon remains, statues, stone pillars, broken banners. At the center of the battlefield, we also erected a stele with three-dimensional character incisions. This is also a part of particular interest to us.

So-called "three-dimensional writing," unlike the two-dimensional writing we commonly use, contains:

Spatial depth: strokes have real depth;

Stroke thickness variation: thick to thin represents "pointing toward the future or uncertain events"; thin to thick represents "historical events that have already occurred";

Overlap of depth and thickness: the thicker and deeper, the more ancient and foundational the recorded event.

The operating logic of another known three-dimensional language is very close to the stele incisions here, so we infer that this is a multidimensional archive format left for "later arrivals" by a higher civilization.

IV. The yellow-sand horizon

The second region is the yellow-sand horizon. According to Jim's subjective account, it was an endless desert, an endless road, and a distant wall of sand. But before building the game, we conducted deep scanning and retrospective reconstruction of this memory, and discovered that under severe pressure and fear, his consciousness had automatically filtered out a large amount of scene information.

In other words: what he saw was not the whole.

We pulled out visual fragments, spatial perception, and faint light and shadow that had not entered conscious awareness, and reconstructed the "outside of the field of vision" in the engine. The result showed that the yellow-sand horizon actually has boundaries. On certain boundary earth walls, caves are arranged regularly. The edges of those cave openings show clear artificial polishing, even an aesthetic tendency. That is to say, this is not a "pure natural desert," but an edge zone modified or used by civilization.

In the game, you will notice one detail: when players leave the main road to explore the boundary, they will gradually see these caves and wall patterns buried by wind and sand. We do not know whether these caves still show signs of civilizational activity, but we can confirm they are not simply background textures. It must be emphasized that in Jim's real experience, he did not find the exit of the yellow-sand horizon.

He was directly "pulled" to the next stage, the rice-field horizon. This means that in the true Moon-dark structure, the "clearance method" for this segment remains blank to us. Therefore, in Another World, we deliberately preserved this blank. The yellow-sand horizon is an area theoretically passable, but so far no player has cleared it. What you see is a map with a deliberate empty space.

V. The rice-field horizon and the little girl

After the yellow-sand horizon came Jim's accident. He was "saved" by the mysterious little girl into the rice-field horizon. The structure here is completely different from before. It possesses:

A stable water system; clearly planned rice fields and crop belts; buildings and dwellings; and at least one intelligent existence far above humanity.

What we can confirm is that the rice fields, houses, farming tools, and irrigation channels all have measurable material spectra and belong to "objective existence." The only exception is the little girl herself.

In Jim's memory, she could touch him, speak with him, and even briefly alter his "falling trajectory." But in all our physical measurement data, no stable material structure corresponding to her was detected. Therefore, in actual research, we temporarily classify her as a kind of "consciousness-matter mixed state."

Her base form is a consciousness entity, but in local high-energy regions, such as the rice-field horizon, she can temporarily manifest through something like "local collapse" into an interactable "quasi-material form."

Under visible light, this form presents the visual and tactile effects of "matter," but in essence it is not an atomic structure in the traditional sense. It is more like a controllable quasi-material interface. This is also the hardest part for us to reconstruct in game design. We cannot truly give the "little girl AI" that kind of authority, so we made a compromise: the little girl players meet in the rice-field horizon is a weakened guide AI. She only guides; she will not "save you out of the world." The authority of the real little girl is far more dangerous.

VI. The frequency horizon and the Terasha Record

Regarding the third region, many people will have a common misunderstanding: "Doesn't the frequency horizon appear only after being sucked into the blue-black sphere in the white horizon? Then isn't the third region the time-slice city?"

Our research found that the "time-slice city" is only one visual projection form of the frequency horizon. The more accurate hierarchy is:

Blue-black sphere: recording interface and index core;

Frequency horizon: the true information field, similar to a "full-time-domain database";

Time-slice city: an interface generated by the frequency horizon for a "consciousness-stream cluster." Combining the little girl's words with Jim's subjective experience, the frequency horizon, the Terasha Record, records all information on Earth since its birth. Note: all information, including material change, energy flow, and consciousness traces.

But this does not mean someone sits there "writing archives." At present, we are more inclined to believe the Terasha Record is a passive recording system, more like a "natural cache layer" of cosmic operation.

All changes in matter, energy, and consciousness leave traces in the frequency domain; the frequency horizon is responsible for "catching" these traces and maintaining a structure that can be queried. The little girl clearly possesses authority to access, index, and even shield part of the record.

At least when Jim attempted to go deeper, she directly terminated access and chose to use "information infusion" to write a large section of "low-order information" into his consciousness, preventing him from being swallowed by the Terasha Record. It was also at that moment that the Spirit Connect pod system suffered a storage overload fault.

The good news is: the fault occurred on our side, because hardware storage was insufficient, not because his consciousness structure collapsed. In a sense, the little girl did save his life. Otherwise, that "information torrent" would very likely have torn him directly into incomplete fragments. In the game, we naturally have no way to freely access the Terasha Record, and have preserved only a distant-view "time-slice city" region to hint at its existence.

VII. The white horizon, the black horizon, and the spacetime well

The white horizon is an extremely uncomfortable transition layer: no boundary, no reference, only blinding white light and that blue-black sphere. We suspect that in the true structure it serves as an "isolation buffer layer," preventing unprepared consciousness from directly falling into the deep frequency horizon. Jim was "sucked" into the Terasha Record from here by the sphere, briefly touched it, then was forcibly ejected back to the white horizon by the little girl. Afterward, he chose to leave the sphere's field of view, which triggered the black horizon.

In our view, the black horizon is the most "higher-order" region currently known. On the surface, it is:

Visually pure black, with no visible-light feedback;

Detectably pure black; even when we extend the spectrum to the current detection limit, we obtain nothing.

According to Jim's experience there, after he touched those "firefly-like information particles," the space began to reveal vortices, structures, even "roads."

Dr. Sean once proposed a model, which everyone should have seen:

Black horizon = naked space + massive dormant "quantum ring-points"

When no consciousness exists, all ring-points remain at a zero ground state. When consciousness enters and issues commands, the ring-points are excited and reorganize into some kind of "experienceable reality." To give a simple analogy: the black horizon itself is a display with no picture; the quantum ring-points are pixels; consciousness is the code that drives the display. Different code, different picture; different consciousness, different world.

This has a dangerous similarity to the principle by which our engine makes Another World. The difference is that we use silicon-based hardware and binary rules, while the "hardware" in the black horizon is far beyond the boundaries of our understanding. More importantly: the black horizon and the spacetime well belong to the same physical field.

The spacetime well itself, judging from our data, is not difficult to understand structurally: it is an information transmission channel, functionally similar to a specialized Spirit Connect pod. But for now, according to my understanding, it transmits only consciousness-structure information and does not transfer matter.

In other words, the spacetime well can send "you as a person" back to your coordinates, but cannot take your body or any physical object along with you.

Jim could return because the well identified and packaged the complete "self-structure," not fragmented memory backups. This is also the baseline conclusion we currently dare state externally:

Consciousness = at least one encodable "information layer" + one temporarily non-replicable "subject core"

The information layer can be transmitted and partially backed up. The subject core can migrate, but cannot be copied. In Another World, the spacetime well is placed at the end of the entire "Moon-dark branch." Any player who reaches it will be forcibly recovered by the system, and the game ends.

At the end of the record, one engineer asked, "What if the player really triggers something, not in the instance, but in the real Moon-dark?"

Dr. Lune did not answer immediately.

The record leaves only his final sentence:

"Then let us not pretend first that we can control everything."

Meeting adjourned.
