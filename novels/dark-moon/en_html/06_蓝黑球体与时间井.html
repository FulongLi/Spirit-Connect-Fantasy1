04 The Blue-Black Sphere and the Spacetime Well

Time slices

After I passed through the new mist, the world around me suddenly darkened. This time, the darkness was not overwhelming; it had order. On both sides of the road, luminous stones were arranged in neat rows. Their brightness was low, but enough to outline the path ahead.

I crouched down and looked carefully at one glowing stone by my foot. Its surface was smooth, yet I could not tell what material it was. More strangely, after I stared at it for a while, its light slowly dimmed, while the other stones outside my field of vision quietly lit up.

It was as if this space were deliberately avoiding being "over-observed."

Compared with the earlier chaos, my state of mind at this moment was unexpectedly calm. Fear comes from the unknown, and once a person begins to catch a pattern, even if it is only an illusion, he temporarily obtains a kind of stability.

I continued forward along the road outlined by these luminous stones. Sure enough, after a certain distance, the mist appeared again, like a buffer zone between two segments of the world.

"Each space has a single theme," I silently summarized as I walked.

"Battlefield, desert, rice field... In each space the field of vision has no visible end, and only one road runs through it. The mist is probably the interface between these spaces." The inference could not be called rigorous, but in the environment of that moment, it was enough to support me in moving on.

After passing through the mist, I arrived in a city.

At first, I almost thought I had returned to some modern metropolis on Earth: tall buildings everywhere, broad streets, smooth metallic luster still on the outer walls of the buildings. Everything looked new and intact.

But after I took the first step forward, things changed. With every step I advanced, the city seemed to age at an accelerated speed. Fine cracks began to appear in the walls, dust covered the glass surfaces, metallic structures lost their shine. Farther on, the cracks widened, outer walls peeled away, windows shattered, and vegetation invaded the buildings.

When concrete and cement no longer have people, nature treats all of it as mountains.

When I walked even farther, the whole city had become broken walls and ruins. I stopped and took a few steps backward.

Everything I had just experienced actually reversed, as if someone had pressed the "rewind" key: ruins reassembled, buildings returned to wholeness, walls restored themselves, glass became clean and bright again.

I stood in place and realized that this city was not a static scene, but the unfolding of an entire time sequence. Every step I took forward pushed that history fast-forward. Every step I took backward rewound it.

When I finally passed through this urban cluster and looked back, the world in my field of vision had become complete wasteland: only incomplete skeletons remained of the towering buildings, and the air was filled with an indescribable pallor. The sky overhead had lost its color; its almost transparent gray-white made the body reject it by instinct. Standing within that scene, I felt a powerful dislocation, as if I had intruded into the endpoint of some civilization.

I did not know what else, besides the spacetime well, would be waiting at the end of this road. But I knew clearly that the existence of this layer of space was itself a declaration: any history that can be recorded can also be fast-forwarded and replayed.

I drew closer to observe, and realized that this space was a domain made of countless "slices." Each slice looked like a suspended thin sheet, yet inside it carried a complete scene: city, wasteland, war, prosperity... Each piece was like a node on a timeline, cut violently out of continuously flowing time, arranged on both sides of the road, stretching upward and downward without end.

This was a vast archive of time!

I reached out and touched one of the slices. The instant my fingertip made contact, the dense surface suddenly opened like liquid, and the details inside rapidly enlarged. I dimly saw a person kneeling on a wasteland. Behind him was a world on the verge of destruction, the earth reflecting wetly, with no birds or clouds anywhere in the sky.

Just as I tried to see that person's face clearly, all the slices in the domain began to roll.

One piece, two pieces, three pieces, countless pieces. Images were stirred, pulled, rotated, and the boundary between reality and memory was shattered in an instant.

I was swept inside.

When I landed, the world had changed.

I stood on that wasteland, with nothing around me. Only the cold, wet ground reflected the pale sky. Between heaven and earth there was no structure that could be clung to. That loneliness was more complete than the desert, colder than the battlefield.

The kneeling figure ahead did not move at all, as if nailed into time.

I tried to approach him. The closer I came, the blurrier his outline became. When I reached out, he was like a projection made of countless particles, easily blown apart by a gust of wind, disappearing cleanly.

"Is this the ending of a civilization?" I asked inwardly.

There was no answer.

When I urgently wished to leave this scene, my vision suddenly turned, and I returned to the center of the slice domain.

The difference was that this time I felt noticeably more oppressed. It was as if mere observation and personal experience had already drawn an invisible boundary in the human way of understanding "ending."

"Spacetime well..." I murmured the words. "I have to find it as soon as possible."

Farther ahead, mist appeared again.

This time, it was no longer gray haze, but an entire mass of pitch-black cloud, with electric light faintly flickering inside it. The black cloud was not simple obstruction. It was more like a structure slowly rotating, like something with self-will, shrouding the entire space.

Standing before the black cloud, I felt a definite impulse to retreat. Reason told me this might be the necessary path into a deeper space. Instinct kept repeating: this is dangerous.

Staying in the wasteland would yield no result.

Forward, at least, still had some kind of direction.

I stretched out my hand and tentatively pushed it into the black cloud. Current passed through my "body" like fine threads, leaving a slight stinging inside consciousness. It was not true pain, but more like noise produced when a frequency band is forcibly pulled.

After confirming that I would not be torn apart immediately, I withdrew my hand, took a deep breath, and stepped fully into the black cloud.

A current that seemed to come from every direction pierced through me at the same time. I heard a high-frequency hum vibrating deep within consciousness.

Then electricity and darkness disappeared together.

The white space and the blue-black sphere

Darkness was replaced by an extreme white.

I arrived in a space that had almost no shadow. I could see no walls and no boundary. Everything was covered by the same dazzling white light. Even the straight road that had run through all the previous worlds was assimilated by that whiteness, nearly impossible to identify from the lines on the ground.

Under such brightness, human visual instinct completely failed. I had to shield my eyes with my hand and barely capture a little contrast through the gaps between my fingers. It was then that I saw it: not far away, a blue-black sphere was suspended in the air.

Its surface was delicate. Its color flowed slowly between deep blue and black, and it seemed to be undergoing irregular expansion and contraction, as if breathing. But the rhythm was chaotic, sometimes fast, sometimes slow, without any pattern that could be modeled.

This thing did not fit with anything around it.

In this space overly simplified into "white," it was the only existence with structure, texture, and signs of life.

I stood where I was and looked at it for a long time.

Curiosity and vigilance fought inside me. To approach it might mean coming into contact with the core rules of this space. To stay away from it might mean remaining forever in a blank world, until my own thoughts were exhausted.

In the end, the former won.

I walked slowly toward the blue-black sphere. When my hand approached its edge, it suddenly stopped all swelling and shrinking. It hung motionless in the air, as if "responding" to this action.

I froze. My heartbeat quickened involuntarily.

None of the structures here seemed random. They were more like a carefully designed response system.

I held my breath, and after a brief silence, lightly touched it.

The sensation was neither solid nor liquid. It was closer to the surface of a high-density colloid. It had no temperature, yet it made me instinctively understand: too many things were carried here. As my gestures changed, the sphere began to shrink, enlarge, and subtly alter its form. Very soon I discovered that, through a certain intuitive "operation," I could control its shape within a limited range. But no matter what I did, I could not unlock any deeper function.

Confusion accumulated little by little inside me. At that moment, a thought suddenly took shape in my consciousness. It was not a sound, nor an image, but a complete segment of "knowing."

"This blue-black sphere is a recorder."

More information followed immediately, as if it had always existed in the depths of my memory and had only just been awakened.

The Moon is tidally locked to Earth. Its bright side is responsible for continuously monitoring Earth. Each orbit constitutes a complete observation cycle. At night, through optical means and other methods not yet named, the Moon captures global images of Earth. All data is filtered and denoised through multiple layers, converted into a form that can be preserved over long periods, and finally gathered into this sphere.

The sphere is an epitome of Earth, not in the geographical sense, but in the sense of a "civilizational and temporal spectrum." It is like the annual rings of a great tree, except they are three-dimensional. Each layer corresponds to an era, and each layer can be indexed. As long as a consciousness entity possesses sufficient authorization and pressure-bearing capacity, it can scale the sphere to the corresponding "era coordinate" and unfold a full-time-domain perspective. Then it can enter that period's "frequency horizon," observing and even experiencing that history as a bystander or embedded participant.

I did not know how I had done it, nor which action had triggered the threshold.

In the next instant, I was drawn into the sphere.

In that new space, I lost all bodily sensation. There were no longer limbs, weight, direction, or even the concepts of "standing" or "lying down." Only one fact remained extremely clear: I existed in the form of "some kind of consciousness structure."

All around me was neither darkness nor light. It was more like an ocean filled with frequencies and information. Countless invisible waveforms surged from the distance and swept past me, carrying immeasurable complexity.

Just as I was about to be submerged by that torrent, a familiar frequency inserted itself.

"I finally found you."

It was her, the voice of the little girl.

"You cannot enter the Terasha Record now." Her voice carried unprecedented seriousness. "You are not ready to travel through the frequency horizon. It will drown you. It will devour you, and in the end you will become part of it."

"The Terasha Record... what is it?" I asked.

"Before you truly enter it, any explanation is futile," she said. "Before that, I need to tell you some things. I will project them into your consciousness-stream frequency band. They will automatically couple with you. When that happens, you will abstractly 'know' part of it. You do not need to memorize it."

As her voice fell, a huge stream of information poured directly into the structure of my consciousness. It was not language. It was more like several civilizations compressed into archives, then violently and forcibly decompressed. My head, if it could still be called a "head," seemed to swell to its limit in an instant.

Interruption

...

"What is this information?" A voice came from an extremely distant place, then suddenly became extraordinarily clear.

It was Dr. Lune.

The feeling of being torn by the torrent vanished abruptly. I was pulled away from the sphere, the white space, and the frequency ocean, returning to a relatively stable perspective of consciousness.

I opened my mouth with difficulty. "I... can't really explain it. Like a very long memory, memories about several civilizations. Every time I try to catch even a little detail, it feels like my brain is about to explode."

"Then let's stop here for today." Dr. Lune deliberately lowered his voice, yet he could not hide his unease. "I'll stop the equipment first. Go back and rest. When you feel ready, we'll continue."

"All right." I agreed. My voice sounded surprisingly calm.

"By the way, do you need the backup of your 'digital self' from before this dual-born connection?" Dr. Lune asked after me.

"We'll talk about it later," I answered weakly.

Immediately afterward, the liquid inside the Spirit Connect pod gradually receded. The hatch slowly opened, and the white laboratory light pierced my eyes again. The difference between returning from a pure information world to a bodily world brought a heaviness that was hard to describe. The body suddenly became very "heavy," and even breathing seemed unnecessary.

"I'm going to the lounge to take a shower and sleep for a while," I told Dr. Lune.

He nodded. His gaze stayed on my face for an instant, as if he wanted to say something. In the end, he only replied simply, "All right. Go first."

I left the test room and walked through those familiar corridors. Automatic doors closed one after another behind me. The slight rush of air each door made as it shut seemed to add another invisible boundary: laboratory and outside world, digital and bodily, memory and reality.

Back in the lounge, I took a quick, mechanical shower and lay down on the bed.

After the lights went out, darkness brought no peace. As soon as I closed my eyes, those images surfaced automatically. Planetary impacts, a great flood falling from the sky, enormous figures standing on the sea like Titans, and also like some race... never recorded in myth. There were continents suspended in the air, or perhaps spacecraft. It was hard to distinguish.

Everything was like a mythic version of cosmic history flipping rapidly through my mind.

"Are these really the memories she gave me?" I asked myself. "Or are the myths I have read and the images I have seen being stirred together during this digital correction?"

The question received no answer. Consciousness sank bit by bit into an unstable state, somewhere between dream and retrospection.

I seemed to experience a war, yet I could never figure out why they were fighting. There were humanoid beings, existences as vast as giant whales, humanoid races that looked like locusts or mantises, humanoid races that looked like lizards, and humanoid races that looked like dragonflies with beating wings behind them. The images kept switching, and there was too much information. No detail could be held firmly. Then the giant whale fell from the sky, its immense shadow covering my entire field of vision. In the instant before it landed, I woke with a start.

I sat up in bed, soaked through, as if I had just been pulled from water.

Drenched in sweat.

I lay back down and tossed from side to side, but I could no longer sleep.

Those images were like some structure implanted deep inside consciousness. The moment I relaxed even slightly, they surfaced automatically.

In the end, I got up, left the lounge, followed the corridor, and returned to the test area.

When I pushed the door open, Dr. Lune was staring at a tablet, carefully observing real-time data waveforms. Those were the status curves of the Spirit Connect pods where Anna and Charlie lay. My sudden appearance startled him. His finger instinctively stopped on the screen.

"Why are you back?" He looked up at me. "Weren't you resting?"

"Can't sleep," I said simply. "Had a dream, or maybe not a dream."

His brows tightened slightly, as if he were assessing my current mental state.

After a moment, he asked, "What do you want to do now?"

"If possible, I want to continue," I said, looking at him. "Continue recalling the journey to the dark side of the Moon. Know the complete experience of my digital self."

He was silent for several seconds. His gaze stayed on my face, moved to the monitor, then returned to me.

"If you are certain, I should be able to do it," he said. "They haven't woken yet. I can have someone else watch them."

There was a restrained excitement in his tone. As a scientist, of course he would not easily let go of such a chance to contact the unknown.

We returned to the test room.

He adjusted the Spirit Connect pod with practiced movements, confirmed the state of the auxiliary equipment, and slid his fingers quickly over the console, checking each monitoring parameter one by one. Once all equipment was ready, he looked at me. There was a sentence in his eyes that he did not say aloud: you can give up at any time.

I nodded and lay back down in the black water.

The journey to the dark side of the Moon was invoked again inside my consciousness.

Black space

Falling into the frequency horizon is an experience almost impossible to describe in words.

When the mysterious little girl pulled my consciousness out of that blue-black sphere, I briefly returned to the pure white space. But that return was incomplete. It was more like being forcibly disconnected from an overloaded system and roughly attached to a backup channel.

By the time I realized this, the little girl was gone.

No matter how I called out in the empty white space, only my own echo answered, vibrating again and again deep within consciousness. The blue-black sphere hung quietly in the distance, returning to its previous kind of chaotic "breathing," swelling and shrinking without rhythm, like a heart, and also like a machine under severely unstable load.

She had left me too much information. The illusion of "seeming to know everything" pressed on the structure of consciousness, creating a dangerous sense of expansion, as if receiving just a little more would make the whole self explode from within.

I stood in place, my eyes never leaving the sphere.

I believed her warning, so I no longer approached. Instead, I began slowly retreating. With every step back, the sphere grew a little smaller in my vision, and the dazzling white light around it subtly changed, as if constantly adjusting exposure.

I stared at it and retreated step by step, until at one moment the sphere vanished completely from my sight.

At that instant, the world beneath my feet collapsed.

The boundless white was rapidly swallowed by a circle of black spreading out from beneath me. This was not light gradually dimming, but "all visible things" being directly stripped away. It was as if someone had turned off the entire display layer of the world, leaving only a pure base color.

Complete darkness.

This darkness was not simply "being unable to see." Even the act of "seeing" itself lost meaning. After vision was sealed, the other senses were forced to magnify, yet darkness was like a filter, weakening touch, hearing, and smell until they were almost nonexistent.

I knew I was standing on some "surface." At least there was still solidity beneath my feet. But that feeling too seemed to come from very far away, and might disappear at any time.

Compared with any previous fall, this time I was closer to true despair. "Compared with all light, I am still more afraid of all black," I said inwardly.

Instinct wanted to call Dr. Lune for help. Of course I knew no one could hear me. To him, at this moment I was only a string of waveforms, a set of constantly changing curves.

Reason at such a time was cold, but useful. I forced myself to calm down and began recalling how my digital self had reached this place. If this had originally been his path, then this darkness must also have an exit.

First, I could confirm that I was not floating in a completely weightless space. I could feel myself "standing." I could lift my foot and walk forward. It was just that, apart from a little support under my soles, all external reference had been erased.

Then, if I had left the white space by "continuously retreating," would walking forward in the opposite direction send me back?

I stepped in the direction before me and walked for a while. Nothing changed. I quickened my pace into a jog. Still nothing changed. Footsteps did not echo here. Even the sensation of my own body's movement was weakened to an almost false degree.

"Of course it won't be that simple," I stopped and panted heavily.

At that moment, one detail suddenly surfaced: I was actually "panting."

In the state where vision and spatial sense had been deprived, breathing became magnified into a clear sense of existence. I began to notice air entering and leaving my lungs, the slight friction of airflow in my throat and nasal cavity.

This explained one thing: at least for the moment, I was still in a simulated "earth-like space," and my senses had been deliberately preserved.

Instead of obsessing over "not seeing," I might as well try not using my eyes.

I closed my eyes, though there was no longer any difference whether I closed them or not, and began tracing back every space I had passed through: stone gate, battlefield, desert, rice field, the city of time slices, black cloud, white space, sphere... The images seemed to project from far away toward me, flashing rapidly through my mind one after another.

Just as I concentrated, the black before me changed.

At first it was only a very faint speck of light, like a single pixel lit on the plane of infinite darkness. Then more and more similar points appeared, slowly connecting into irregular arcs and spirals, like a still meteor shower suspended around me.

I opened my eyes. This time, "opening my eyes" regained meaning.

I found myself at the center of a huge vortex.

The vortex rotated slowly around me as its origin. Points of light circled around me ring by ring, brightening and dimming, like countless fireflies resonating under some invisible rhythm.

I tried reaching out to touch those light points. The instant my fingertip brushed against one cluster of light, it burst into a stream of information, like a small electric current rushing directly into my consciousness.

It was one piece of information the girl had left me, not a specific sentence, but a whole method for "locating space by intention."

This black space was not divided by physical coordinates, but mapped through "imagery": as long as there was a sufficiently clear scene in consciousness, the space would generate the corresponding "exit" in that direction.

What I think is where I arrive.

Within that same information, she had also given me an image of the structure of the space where the spacetime well was located: a high platform, a throne, and an entrance like a solidified wave surface.

I closed my eyes and constructed that image in my mind.

When I opened my eyes again, the vortex, the light points, and the darkness all withdrew. In their place stood a huge throne rising in the void.

The spacetime well

Seen from far away, the spacetime well did not look like a well at all. It was more like a throne, set upon an empty platform. Behind the throne was a continuously twisting piece of space. It was not a wall, nor a door, but a constantly deforming geometric structure. Light was stretched and folded within it, slowly stirred like liquid.

I stood at a distance and looked for a long time.

"Do I just... walk in?" I asked inwardly.

The throne did not respond, and the space showed no dynamic change, as if it were only patiently waiting for an event that was bound to occur.

I began to approach.

The closer I came to the throne, the more obvious the distortion behind it became. The stretched light separated from normal three-dimensional geometry, forming shapes that could not be described by existing concepts, like the afterimages of high-dimensional objects forcibly projected into three dimensions, carrying a distortion that deliberately "preserved information."

Reason was pushed little by little toward its limit here. Human intuition, experience, and scientific knowledge, before such a scene, were almost left with only one outcome: silence. I tried to give myself an explanation. Perhaps this was a visualized presentation of an "algorithmic interface." Perhaps the so-called spacetime well was, in essence, a system for reading, judging, and assigning.

The moment I arrived before the throne, an extremely low-frequency vibration came from beneath my feet. It was not sound, but more like a rhythmic inquiry, echoing through bones, nerves, and even the structure of consciousness.

Thump thump... thump thump...

The world seemed to come alive in that instant.

Or more precisely, something that had always existed began, in that instant, to look directly at me. It was not hostility, nor goodwill, but a calm and objective "scrutiny." I suddenly realized that in the eyes of this system, I was not a "visitor," but an "input sample."

Some questions were thrown at me without language: Who are you? Why did you come here? Do you understand what you have already seen?

I did not know in what form I should answer, nor was I certain I had the right to "refuse."

So I chose to keep walking.

With every step forward, the world behind me grew farther away, while the distortion behind the throne came closer. That distortion was not a visual illusion, but a real "cognitive tearing." I could feel my capacity for understanding being stretched to its limit, then approaching the edge of collapse little by little.

Just when I felt that one more step would tear my whole being apart, a voice sounded softly deep within consciousness: "Do not enter with emotion."

It was her voice, the little girl who had spoken with me in the rice field.

I froze, almost wanting to turn back and search for her figure. But in the space there was only the throne, only the distorted entrance, no one at all.

"Is that you?" I asked. "Are you still here?"

There was no answer.

Only that soft reminder echoed again and again deep inside consciousness: do not enter with emotion.

I suddenly understood something. The spacetime well did not distinguish between "dangerous" and "not dangerous." It merely executed a calm set of rules.

The true risk came from the state of consciousness at the moment of entry. Any extreme emotion might be treated by this system as an "abnormal parameter," amplified, distorted, or even dismantled during reconstruction.

I stood where I was and suddenly laughed.

The laugh was not joyful. It was more like helpless self-mockery. Here, I was nothing more than a string of complex data that could still be read and written.

I closed my eyes and tried as much as possible to steady my heartbeat, tried as much as possible to let go of the fear and unease I had just experienced. It was not a matter of convincing myself that there was "no danger." It was, on some level, admitting that whether danger existed or not, I had already reached this place.

When I opened my eyes again, the space behind the throne was no longer twisting so violently.

The entrance was like a quiet surface of water, its ripples extremely shallow, as if waiting only for a single finger to touch it and produce a chain reaction.

I took the first step, the second, the third.

I walked into that light. There was no tearing, no pain, no blazing white flash.

Everything was emptied in an instant.

The world disappeared.

The body disappeared too.

Only a string of information remained, burning like thunder deep inside my consciousness:

You did not come to explore. You came to return memory.

This sentence did not seem to be spoken to me. It was more like an annotation made to the entire system, defining my role in this journey. When "I" once again possessed the concept of "consciousness," I was standing in an empty room.

It was the room of my digital self.

Familiar, yet empty. Bleak, desolate, like a node quietly cleared out. Before I could confirm anything further, everything fell into darkness once again.

Return

When I woke again, what I saw was the familiar translucent hatch of the Spirit Connect pod.

The cold white light of the Spirit Connect experience room passed through the glass and reflected on my retina, pressing reality back down into a calculable scale.

Breathing became real again. Gravity pressed down on the body again. Heartbeats traveled through the blood vessels to the fingertips. My stomach felt awful. All these bodily sensations unexpectedly brought a strange sense of safety.

Before my vision had fully focused, I heard someone speaking outside the glass.

"Jim's consciousness is back! Quick... notify the medical team!"

It was Dr. Lune's voice, slightly distorted through the pod wall, yet unable to hide its tension and excitement. He shouted while keeping his eyes fixed on the monitor.

The hatch slowly rose. Cold air poured in. Strangely, I was not soaking in black water. For the first time in the true sense, I had "returned to the real world."

My throat was dry. When I spoke, my voice was unstable. To my own surprise, the first sentence I said was not about the sights on the dark side of the Moon, nor about civilization, the spacetime well, or that blue-black sphere.

I heard myself ask, "My digital self... is he still there? And that little girl... is she still there too?"

The surroundings briefly fell silent.

Dr. Lune did not answer at once. He only stared at me, his eyes too complicated to summarize with any single emotion. There was the curiosity of a scientist, the awe of an onlooker, and a faint caution that was hard to detect.

After a long time, he slowly spoke. "Jim, in your record, we discovered a frequency never seen before."

He paused, as if weighing each word.

"We don't know who you were talking to, nor do we know what dimension you were in... or what you saw. We can only confirm one thing."

He stared at me. In his eyes flashed a light I had never seen before.

"You brought back some unknown... information structure."

He deliberately lowered his voice. "And it is not yours. Nor is it your digital self's."

The air became unusually heavy in that moment.

I said nothing. I had not escaped from the spacetime well. I had been sent back. Not because I wanted to return, but because some existence allowed me to return, and perhaps even needed me to return. And the fragmented images and memories I now possessed were merely the necessary and controllable remnants it had left inside my consciousness.

Dr. Lune slowly exhaled, as if he had finally made some decision.

"Jim..." he said softly. "We need to talk."
