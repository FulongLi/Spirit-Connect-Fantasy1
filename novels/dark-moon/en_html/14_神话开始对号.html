12 The Myths Begin to Match

Mr. Speer spoke. "Then where does the human soul come from?"

His voice was calm, but the knuckles on the tabletop tightened slightly. Jim saw that movement, though he could not say why he remembered it.

Before Jim could speak, Jared had already given a light cough and taken the initiative. "From the perspective of mythological systems, the 'soul' is never created. It is placed, summoned, or 'sent.'"

He turned to Jim. "How did she put it?"

Jim's gaze darkened slightly. "She said they did not create souls. They only did something very simple, and very cruel..."

He raised his head and looked at everyone. "They manufactured a container for souls that already existed. As for where souls came from, she did not tell me, or did not project it to me. But I believe the answer should be in the Terasha Record."

At the other end of the conference table, someone said softly, "In other words, intelligent life is not a biological miracle. Humans are hosts for consciousness."

Jim nodded lightly. "Yes."

As soon as he finished, Jared had already turned the paper in his hand to the next page and added softly, "From the perspective of archaeology and mythological genealogy, this claim... is extremely dangerous, but also extremely consistent."

Mr. Speer raised an eyebrow. "Jared, would you care to expand on that?"

Ancient gods

Jared nodded, attached the magnetic disk, and lightly swiped his hand across the tabletop to call up an auxiliary projection.

"Before Jim spoke, you invited me here because you wanted me to do one thing: see whether his account corresponds with known ancient civilizational legends on Earth. The simple answer is: it does, and far more than I expected."

The projection successively displayed cuneiform tablets, Egyptian murals, Maya reliefs, oracle-bone inscriptions, and ancient totems.

"Let us begin with Sumerian civilization." Jared pushed up his glasses, and the projection showed a clay tablet covered in cuneiform. "In Sumerian myth, the Anunnaki descended from heaven and came to Earth from the planet Nibiru. They did not come to conquer, but to 'work,' to mine gold, because Nibiru's atmosphere required gold for repair. But the Anunnaki grew tired of heavy labor, so they decided to 'create' a being to work in their place."

The projection switched to an ancient relief: a deity shaping a human form.

"The key is here: they did not create from nothing, but 'mixed.' They mixed the divinity of the Anunnaki, namely blood, with Earth's 'primitive worker,' possibly apes. In terms of linguistic structure, the word used in Sumerian texts is 'create,' but a more accurate translation should be 'modify' or 'intervene.' They granted these new beings wisdom and language, allowing them to understand commands, use tools, and carry out complex labor."

The projection switched again, this time to images from Chinese civilization.

"In ancient Chinese myth, Nuwa molded humans from yellow earth and gave them life. But in older versions, Nuwa does not 'create' humanity; she 'repairs the sky,' repairing some systemic defect. Her process of making humans is more like mass production: at first she carefully shaped them by hand, then later dipped a vine in muddy water and flicked it out, hence the distinction between noble and lowly humans. But regardless of version, they share one point: humans are 'given' something. Spirituality, wisdom, or what one might call a 'soul.'"

"More interestingly, Chinese mythology also has the figure of Fuxi. He taught humans to weave nets and fish, domesticate livestock, invent writing, and establish calendars. He is not a creator, but an 'educator.'"

Images of Maya civilization appeared on the projection.

"In Maya and Mesoamerican myths, gods create humans multiple times. This is a very explicit 'iteration' process. The first humans are made of mud, but they are weak and are washed away by water. The second are made of wood, but they have no souls and no memory, and are ultimately destroyed by a flood. The third are made from white maize and yellow maize, and this time they succeed. These three failures and one success, viewed from a modern perspective, look exactly like version iteration in experiments, adjusting parameters continuously until the correct formula is found."

He paused, and ancient Egyptian images appeared on the projection.

"In ancient Egyptian myth, Ptah shapes humanity from clay. But more importantly, he also creates language, art, architecture, and law. He is not simply making humans. He is establishing an entire civilizational system. And the stories of demigod-kings like Horus and Osiris share a common pattern: descending from heaven, transmitting knowledge, establishing order, then leaving or 'ascending.'"

More civilizational images appeared on the projection: India, Greece, the Norse world, the Inca...

"In Indian myth, Brahma creates humanity, but more accurately, he 'assigns' human castes and duties. In Greek myth, Prometheus forms humans from clay and steals heavenly fire to grant them wisdom. In Norse myth, Odin creates humans from two trees and gives them souls. In Inca myth, Viracocha creates humans, then teaches them agriculture, architecture, astronomy..."

He paused, with a trace of excitement in his eyes. "What is even more interesting is that almost every ancient civilization has a common module: 'people who come from heaven,' 'educators,' 'demigod-kings.' These figures do not create the universe. They are responsible for only one thing: modifying humans, making humans into humans rather than animals, granting language, law, agriculture, metallurgy, astronomy, and calendars. And these 'educators' share one common feature: the knowledge they transmit often exceeds the understanding ability of humans at that time. For example, the Sumerians possessed precise astronomical calendars and could predict solar and lunar eclipses, but their mathematical level was insufficient to explain where such knowledge came from. The Maya built precise calendar systems capable of calculating celestial phenomena thousands of years later, but that knowledge seems to appear overnight. The ancient Egyptians possessed precise architectural techniques capable of building pyramids, but the origin of those techniques cannot be traced."

He looked up at Mr. Speer and said, "If we treat these myths as blurred memories of 'some kind of intervention,' Jim's explanation just now becomes highly consistent. These 'educators' were very likely 'engineers' sent by a civilization. Their goal was not conquest, but modification and guidance."

The meeting room fell into brief silence.

Finally, someone could not help muttering, "So you are using myth to support a memory from the Moon?"

"Precisely the opposite." Jared smiled slightly. "I am saying that before Jim opened his mouth, within 'Earth's own memory system,' similar structures had already been preserved. It is only that no one dared take them seriously."

He tapped his forehead. "These things did not disappear. They were only compressed into symbols and stories, hidden in the place we call 'culture.'"

Jim quietly watched all this and suddenly realized one thing: what he described was "memory from beyond Earth," while what Jared presented was "memory from within Earth."

And at this moment, these two sets of memory were matching each other in a dangerous way.
