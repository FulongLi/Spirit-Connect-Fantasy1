21 The Roswell Inheritance

The crashed spacecraft

After a brief silence, Dr. Lune spoke. "Professor Cross, I want to hear you talk about that military section... about the so-called crashed alien spacecraft, and the true origin of IHinDiA."

"You now call it the International Humanity Digitalisation Agency, IHinDiA." Professor Cross sighed. "Publicly, it is 'a platform for humanity's joint management of consciousness technology.' But for a long time before the war, it was only a buffer between the military and major conglomerates."

"More than a hundred years ago, an unknown alien spacecraft crashed in a place whose name we no longer mention."

He paused. "The key is not the crash itself, but what was passed over in one line in the materials you later saw..."

"The ship's control module," Dr. Lune said softly.

"Yes."

"They opened the outer shell, dismantled the cabin walls, removed every layer of protection. Finally, in the spacecraft's 'piloting area,' what they saw was not a steering lever, not buttons, but an extremely complex array of consciousness interfaces. That spacecraft was directly controlled by some kind of 'consciousness structure.'"

"So what did they do?"

"Reverse engineering," Dr. Sean said softly.

"Reverse engineering," Professor Cross repeated. "Not of the spacecraft itself, but of that consciousness interface array. They began studying how consciousness could be drawn out of or copied from the body, and how it could be 'written into' a completely different carrier. This was one of the original sources of the later 'consciousness extraction and digital mapping' technology you saw."

He gestured with his hand. "Of course, it was not all credit to aliens. Humanity's own accumulation in neuroscience, quantum perception, and cognitive modeling was thrown into the same furnace. Without that knowledge, no matter how much reverse engineering we did, we would not have understood its working principles. The military only did one thing: after hiding it for so many years, it finally disclosed the truth to the world."

"So an organization like IHinDiA had to appear as a buffer," Dr. Lune said. "Otherwise, the party unilaterally holding this technology would become a global target, especially after the war."

"The military knew this, and capital knew it even more clearly," Professor Cross said. "If only the military possessed the means of consciousness extension, then capital's dream of immortality would have nothing to do with them. So they reached a rare consensus: release the technology 'downward,' hang up a sign saying 'shared progress for all humanity,' and let a transnational organization serve as gatekeeper."

"Add one more condition: every institution using this architecture must regularly transmit 'non-sensitive data' back," Dr. Lune smiled bitterly. "No one has ever clearly defined non-sensitive."

"What do you call it internally?"

"Feeding the monster."

Professor Cross laughed. "Quite accurate."

He pointed at the projected cross-section of the Spirit Connect pod on the tabletop. "Once a bit of an 'incomplete version' of the technology was thrown out of the black box, companies like yours were pushed onto the stage. Virtual reality changed from toy to scalpel; memory correction changed from psychological counseling into writing operations on the bottom layer of consciousness."

"At first, we only wanted to help those with disabilities repair a few 'cracks,'" Dr. Lune smiled bitterly. "Now we find that we ourselves have followed along to stand at the edge of the crack and look down."
