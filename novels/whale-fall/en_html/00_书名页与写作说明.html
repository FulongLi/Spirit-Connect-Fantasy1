00 Title Page and Writing Notes

Chapter Position

  • Part: frontmatter
  • Target length: 1,500-2,000 Chinese characters in the source draft
  • Function: Establish the V1 writing target, chapter-file rules, and bilingual reading mode for The Falling of the Giant Whale.

Draft Seed

This is not the final manuscript. It is an engineering version that can be expanded chapter by chapter. Each chapter file preserves its dramatic function, points where civilization background may enter, and a seed of prose. Later expansion should follow natural causality, so characters do not stop inside a scene merely to explain setting.

Writing Notes

  • Let events happen naturally. Write the body first, then the choice, then let civilization background surface through the scene.
  • Avoid encyclopedia-style exposition. Background information should appear through ruins, wounds, decrees, songs, escape routes, and mistaken understanding.
  • Charlie's core nature is ordinary: he fears death, talks too much, and often wants to retreat. At the decisive moment, he still follows. He does not suddenly become a hero; he slowly learns how to witness.
  • The core second-draft aesthetic is inevitability. Major events should feel as if there was no other path. Characters resist first, their exits close one by one, and their final choice hurts them while still matching who they are.
  • The emotional climax in war should not be written as a single human romance. It should show a cross-species commonality: the willingness to limit oneself, to give up possession, so the future does not have to become a copy of oneself.
  • The material connected to Lingmao should remain only as a faint afterwave, not as a hard setup for this book. The ending emotion of The Falling of the Giant Whale should stay with the blue whale song and the pain Charlie brings back.

Civilization Insert

This chapter inserts only the necessary background. It should not explain the whole truth too early. The reader should be disoriented with Charlie first, then gradually understand Phaeton's final war, the Giant Whale people, and the origin of Earth's inheritance.
